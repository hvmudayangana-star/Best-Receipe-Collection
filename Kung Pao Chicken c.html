Kung Pao Chicken

Kung Pao Chicken is a popular Chinese stir-fried dish from Sichuan cuisine.

Key points about Kung Pao Chicken:

  • Made with diced chicken, peanuts, and dried red chilies.
  • Cooked in a savory, slightly sweet and spicy sauce.
  • Flavored with soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns.
  • Has a balance of spicy, salty, and nutty flavors.

Kung Pao Chicken is usually served hot with steamed rice and is enjoyed worldwide.

Ingredients

    For the chicken:

  • 300g boneless chicken (cut into small cubes)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp Shaoxing wine or dry sherry (optional)

    For the sauce:

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce (for color, optional)
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 3 tbsp water

    Other ingredients:

  • 3–4 dried red chilies (broken in half, seeds removed for less heat)
  • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 tsp ginger (minced)
  • ¼ cup roasted peanuts
  • 2–3 spring onions (chopped into 1-inch pieces)
  • 2 tbsp oil for stir-frying

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken:
    • Mix chicken with soy sauce, cornstarch, and wine. Let sit for 10–15 mins.

  2. Prepare sauce:
    • Mix all sauce ingredients in a bowl and set aside.

  3. Stir-fry:
    • Heat oil in a wok or pan.
    • Add dried chilies and stir for a few seconds until fragrant.
    • Add garlic and ginger, then the marinated chicken. Stir-fry until cooked through.

  4. Add sauce:
    • Pour in the prepared sauce. Stir until it thickens and coats the chicken.

  5. Finish:
    • Add peanuts and spring onions. Toss for 30 seconds and remove from heat.

Serve hot with steamed rice.
