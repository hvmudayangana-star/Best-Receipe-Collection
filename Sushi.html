Sushi

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish made mainly with vinegared rice, combined with a variety of ingredients.

Key points about Sushi:

  • The rice is seasoned with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt.
  • Topped or filled with raw or cooked seafood, vegetables, or egg.
  • Often served with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.
  • Known for its fresh, delicate flavors and neat presentation.

Common types of Sushi:

  • Nigiri – rice topped with fish or seafood
  • Maki – rice and fillings rolled in seaweed (nori)
  • Sashimi – sliced raw fish (no rice)
  • Temaki – hand-rolled cone-shaped sushi

Sushi is popular worldwide and is eaten as a main meal or light dish.

Ingredients

    For the Sushi rice:

  • cup sushi rice (short-grain rice)
  • 1¼ cups water
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • ½ tsp salt

    For the Sushi Roll:

  • 4–5 sheets of nori (seaweed)
  • 1 small cucumber (cut into thin strips)
  • 1 small carrot or avocado (optional)
  • Fresh fish (like salmon or tuna) or cooked crab sticks
  • Soy sauce (for dipping)
  • Wasabi & pickled ginger (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the rice:
    • Rinse sushi rice until water runs clear. Cook with water
    • Let it steam, then cool slightly.

  2. Season the rice:
    • Mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt.
    • Gently mix into the cooked rice.

  3. Prepare filling:
    • Slice veggies and fish into thin strips.

  4. Assemble the roll:
    • Place a nori sheet on a bamboo mat (shiny side down).
    • Spread a thin layer of rice over the nori, leaving 1 inch at the top.
    • Lay fillings in a line across the center.

  5. Roll tightly:
    • Use the mat to roll from the bottom, pressing gently. Seal edge with a bit of water.

  6. Cut and serve:
    • Cut with a wet sharp knife into 6–8 pieces.
    • Serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.
