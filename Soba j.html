Soba

Soba is a traditional Japanese noodle made mainly from buckwheat flour

Key points about Soba:

  • Thin, long noodles with a light brown color.
  • Have a mild, slightly nutty flavor.
  • Can be eaten hot or cold, depending on the dish.
  • Often served with soy-based broth, spring onions, and wasabi.

Common types of Soba dishes:

  • Zaru soba – cold soba served with dipping sauce
  • Kake soba – hot soba in broth
  • Tempura soba – soba served with tempura

Takoyaki originated in Osaka, Japan, and is famous for its savory taste and texture.

Ingredients

    For the noodles:

  • 200g dried soba noodles
  • Water (for boiling)
  • Ice (for chilling)

    For the dipping sauce (Tsuyu):

  • 1 cup dashi stock (or substitute with light fish/veggie broth)
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup mirin (or 1 tbsp sugar + water if unavailable)

    Optional Toppings:

  • Chopped green onions
  • Grated daikon (white radish)
  • Nori (dried seaweed strips)
  • Wasabi (small amount)

Instructions

  1. Boil the soba noodles:
    • Bring water to a boil, add soba noodles.
    • Cook for 5–7 minutes (check package).
    • Drain and rinse under cold running water to remove starch.
    • Place in ice water to chill.

  2. Make the dipping sauce (Tsuyu):
    • Combine dashi, soy sauce, and mirin in a small pan.
    • Bring to a quick boil, then cool. Refrigerate until chilled.

  3. Serve:
    • Drain noodles and place on a plate or bamboo mat.
    • Serve dipping sauce separately in a small bowl.
    • Add toppings as desired.

Eat by dipping cold noodles into the chilled sauce.
