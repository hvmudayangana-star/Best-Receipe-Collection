String Hoppers

String hoppers (called Idiyappam / ඉඩියප්පම්) are a traditional Sri Lankan and South Indian food made from rice flour.

Key points about String Hoppers:

  • Made by steaming thin rice-flour noodles pressed into round mats.
  • Soft, light, and easy to digest.
  • Commonly eaten for breakfast or dinner.
  • Usually served with coconut sambol (pol sambol), dhal curry, chicken or fish curry, or kirihodi (coconut milk gravy).

String hoppers are a staple food in Sri Lankan homes and are very popular for both everyday meals and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups roasted rice flour (or regular rice flour, sifted)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1½ to 2 cups hot water (as needed)

Instructions

  1. Mix the Dough:
    • In a bowl, combine rice flour and salt.
    • Gradually add hot water and mix with a spoon (then hand) until a soft, non-sticky dough forms.

  2. Prepare the String Hoppers:
    • Fill the dough into a string hopper press or idiyappam maker.
    • Press the dough into thin noodle shapes onto greased string hopper mats or banana leaves (in circular shapes).

  3. Steam:
    • Place mats in a steamer.
    • Steam for about 8–10 minutes until cooked (they'll look firm and slightly glossy).

  4. Serve with Coconut sambol, dhal curry, or kiri hodi (coconut milk gravy)
