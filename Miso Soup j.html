Miso Soup

Miso soup is a traditional Japanese soup made with a fermented soybean paste called miso.

Key points about Miso Soup:

  • The base is dashi broth (made from seaweed or fish).
  • Miso paste is stirred in, giving the soup a salty, umami flavor.
  • Common ingredients include tofu cubes, seaweed (wakame), and spring onions.
  • Usually served as a side dish with meals in Japan.

Miso soup is light, healthy, and commonly eaten for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dashi stock (or light vegetable broth)
  • 2 tbsp miso paste (white or red)
  • ½ cup tofu (cubed, soft or medium firm)
  • 1–2 tbsp wakame seaweed (dried, soaked in water for 5 mins)
  • 1–2 tbsp green onions (chopped)

Instructions

  1. Heat the dashi stock in a pot over medium heat. Do not boil.
  2. Add soaked wakame and tofu cubes. Simmer gently for 2–3 minutes.
  3. Dissolve miso paste:
    • In a small bowl, mix miso with a few spoonfuls of hot broth until smooth.
    • Then add this mixture back to the pot.

  4. Stir gently and heat through (do not let it boil after adding miso, as it affects flavor and nutrients).
  5. Add chopped green onions just before serving.

Serve hot.Light, healthy, and perfect as a starter or side dish!
