Hot Pot

Hot pot is a popular East Asian communal meal where people cook food together at the table.

Key points about Hot Pot:

  • A pot of simmering broth is placed in the center of the table.
  • Diners add raw ingredients such as thinly sliced meat, seafood, vegetables, tofu, and noodles.
  • Food cooks quickly in the broth and is eaten fresh.
  • Usually eaten with dipping sauces like soy sauce, sesame sauce, or chili oil.
  • Broths can be mild or very spicy, especially in Chinese hot pot.

Hot pot is enjoyed as a social and sharing meal, especially in cold weather.

Ingredients

    For the Proteins:

  • Thinly sliced beef, chicken, lamb
  • Shrimp, fish fillets, squid rings
  • Tofu (firm or silken)
  • Fish balls or meatballs

    For the Vegetables:

  • Napa cabbage, spinach, bok choy
  • Mushrooms (enoki, shiitake, oyster)
  • Lotus root, taro slices
  • Corn on the cob (cut small)

    Noodles:

  • Glass noodles, rice noodles, or udon

    Others:

  • Bean sprouts
  • Tofu skin
  • Seaweed

Instructions

  1. Heat the broth in a pot or electric hot pot at the table.
  2. Add ingredients as you eat — they cook in 1–5 minutes.
  3. Dip cooked items into your sauce and enjoy with rice or noodles.
