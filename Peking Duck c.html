Peking Duck

Peking Duck is a famous traditional Chinese dish from Beijing.

Key points about Peking Duck:

  • Made from roast duck with crispy skin and tender meat.
  • The duck is specially prepared and roasted until the skin becomes golden and crunchy.
  • Served in thin slices with pancakes, spring onions, cucumber, and hoisin sauce.
  • Eaten by wrapping the duck in the pancake with the accompaniments.

Peking Duck is considered a luxury and celebratory dish and is well known around the world for its rich flavor and crispy skin.

Ingredients

    For the duck:

  • 1 whole duck (about 2–2.5 kg)
  • 2 tbsp Chinese five-spice powder
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey or maltose
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • Salt

    For glazing:

  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp hot water
  • 1 tbsp vinegar

    To serve:

  • Mandarin pancakes or thin flatbreads
  • Spring onions (cut into strips)
  • Cucumber (cut into strips)
  • Hoisin sauce or sweet bean sauce

Instructions

  1. Prep the duck:
    • Clean and pat dry the duck. Rub inside and out with five-spice powder and salt.

  2. Air-dry the duck:
    • Mix soy sauce, vinegar, hoisin, and honey. Brush over the duck skin.
    • Let it air-dry uncovered in the fridge for 8–24 hours for crispy skin.

  3. Glaze & roast:
    • Before roasting, brush the duck with the honey + vinegar glaze.
    • Roast in a preheated oven at 190°C (375°F) for 1.5–2 hours, basting occasionally.

  4. Rest & slice:
    • Let the duck rest, then thinly slice the crispy skin and meat.

  5. Serve:
    • In pancakes with spring onions, cucumber, and hoisin sauce.
