Kokis

Kokis (කොකිස්) is a traditional Sri Lankan sweet snack made from rice flour and coconut milk.

Key points about Kokis:

  • Prepared using a special kokis mould dipped in hot oil.
  • The batter-coated mould is fried until crispy and golden.
  • Has a crunchy texture and mildly sweet taste.
  • Commonly made during Sinhala and Tamil New Year and festive occasions.

Kokis is a beloved festive snack in Sri Lanka, often served with tea.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • ¼ cup coconut milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1–2 tbsp all-purpose flour (optional, for crispiness)
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder (for color)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Kokis mould (iron mould is essential)

Instructions

  1. Mix rice flour, coconut milk, egg, turmeric, salt, and optional plain flour into a smooth, slightly thick batter.
  2. Let it rest for 10–15 minutes.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan.
  4. Place the kokis mould in hot oil for a few minutes to heat it up.
  5. Dip the hot mould into the batter (only ¾ of the way—don’t fully submerge).
  6. Then immediately place it in the hot oil.
  7. After a few seconds, gently shake the mould to release the kokis into the oil.
  8. Let it fry until crisp and golden.
  9. Remove and drain on paper towels.

As a crunchy tea-time snack, especially during Sinhala & Tamil New Year.
