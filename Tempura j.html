Tempura

Tempura is a Japanese cooking style where food is lightly battered and deep-fried.

Key points about Tempura:

  • Uses a thin batter made from flour, cold water, and egg.
  • Fried quickly in hot oil, making it crispy and light, not oily.
  • Common ingredients include shrimp, fish, vegetables (like pumpkin, sweet potato, eggplant).
  • Usually served with a dipping sauce (tentsuyu) made from soy sauce, dashi, and mirin.

Tempura is enjoyed as a main dish, side dish, or topping for noodles like udon or soba.

Ingredients

    For the batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg (cold)
  • 1 cup ice-cold water
  • (Optional: 1–2 tbsp cornstarch for extra crispiness)

    For the tempura items (your choice):

  • Vegetables:
    • sweet potato
    • carrot
    • bell pepper
    • eggplant
    • zucchini
    • green beans
  • Seafood
    • shrimp
    • squid
    • white fish

    For frying

  • Oil (Vegetable or canola)

    • For dipping sauce (Tentsuyu):

  • ½ cup dashi or light fish stock
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin

Instructions

  1. Prepare ingredients:
    • Slice vegetables thinly and pat dry.
    • If using shrimp, devein and slightly score the belly to prevent curling.

  2. Make the batter:
    • Beat the egg in a bowl, add cold water.
    • Gently mix in flour with chopsticks or a fork (do NOT overmix; lumps are okay).
    • Keep batter cold by placing the bowl over ice.

  3. Heat oil:
    • Heat oil in a deep pan to 170–180°C (340–355°F).
  4. Dip & Fry:
    • Lightly coat items in flour, then dip into batter.
    • Fry a few pieces at a time until light golden (1–2 mins).
    • Remove and drain on paper towels.
  5. Make dipping sauce:
    • Mix dashi, soy sauce, and mirin. Warm slightly or serve at room temp.

Serve immediately with dipping sauce. Enjoy the crispiness! Let me know if you want a vegetarian version.
