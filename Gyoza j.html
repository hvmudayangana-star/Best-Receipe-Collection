Gyoza

Gyoza are Japanese dumplings made with thin wrappers and a savory filling.

Key points about Gyoza:

  • Filled with minced meat (usually pork or chicken) and finely chopped vegetables like cabbage, garlic, and spring onions.
  • Commonly pan-fried, then lightly steamed (called yaki-gyoza).
  • Crispy on the bottom and soft on top.
  • Served with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, and chili oil.

Gyoza are popular as a side dish, snack, or appetizer in Japanese cuisine.

Ingredients

    For the filling:

  • 200g ground pork or chicken
  • 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)
  • 2 spring onions (chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

    For wrapping:

  • 20 gyoza wrappers (store-bought or homemade)

    For cooking:

  • 1–2 tbsp oil
  • ¼ cup water (for steaming)

    For dipping sauce:

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • (Optional: chili oil or minced garlic)

Instructions

  1. Make the filling:
    • Combine pork, cabbage, ginger, garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, spring onions, salt & pepper. Mix well.

  2. Fill the wrappers:
    • Place 1 tsp filling in the center of each wrapper.
    • Wet the edge with water, fold in half, and pinch the edges to seal (pleat if desired).

  3. Cook the gyoza:
    • Heat oil in a nonstick pan.
    • Place gyoza flat side down. Fry until bottoms are golden brown (2–3 mins).
    • Add ¼ cup water and cover to steam for 4–5 mins until cooked through.
    • Uncover and let the bottoms crisp again.

  4. Serve hot with dipping sauce.

Serve hot.Light, healthy, and perfect as a starter or side dish!
