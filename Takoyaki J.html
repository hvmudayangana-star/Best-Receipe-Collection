Takoyaki

Takoyaki is a popular Japanese street food made from small, round batter balls.

Key points about Takoyaki:

  • Made from a wheat-flour batter cooked in a special round-mold pan.
  • Filled with pieces of octopus (tako), spring onions, and pickled ginger.
  • Crispy on the outside and soft inside.
  • Topped with takoyaki sauce, mayonnaise, bonito flakes, and seaweed powder.
  • Commonly eaten as a snack or street food.

Takoyaki originated in Osaka, Japan, and is famous for its savory taste and texture.

Ingredients

    For the batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups dashi stock (or water with a dash of soy sauce)
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • A pinch of salt

    For the filling :

  • 100–150g cooked octopus (cut into small cubes)
  • 2–3 tbsp green onions (chopped)
  • Pickled ginger (beni shoga), finely chopped (optional)
  • Tempura scraps (tenkasu) – optional, for crunch

    For toppings :

  • Takoyaki sauce (or substitute with thick soy sauce + ketchup)
  • Japanese mayo
  • Bonito flakes (katsuobushi)
  • Aonori (dried seaweed flakes)

Instructions

  1. Make the batter:
    • Whisk flour, eggs, dashi stock, soy sauce, salt, and baking powder until smooth.

  2. Heat takoyaki pan:
    • Oil the pan well. Heat over medium.

  3. Cook the balls:
    • Pour batter into each round hole (fill to the top).
    • Add a piece of octopus, green onions, ginger, and tempura scraps into each.

  4. Flip & cook:
    • Once the edges set, use a skewer to turn each ball 90°, then again until round.
    • Cook until golden brown and crisp outside.

  5. Serve:
    • Drizzle with takoyaki sauce and mayo.
    • Sprinkle with bonito flakes and aonori.
