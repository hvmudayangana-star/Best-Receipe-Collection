Wonton Soup

Wonton Soup is a traditional Chinese soup made with wontons served in a light, flavorful broth.

Key points about Wonton Soup:

  • Wontons are small dumplings filled with minced meat (usually pork or chicken) and sometimes shrimp.
  • The soup has a clear broth, often made from chicken stock.
  • Commonly includes spring onions, greens, and sometimes noodles.
  • Light, comforting, and not very spicy.

Wonton soup is popular as a starter or light meal and is commonly served in Chinese restaurants worldwide.

Ingredients

    For Wontons:

  • Wonton wrappers
  • 200 g minced chicken or pork
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped spring onions
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • ½ tsp ginger (grated)
  • 1 clove garlic (finely chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

    For the sauce:

  • 4 cups chicken stock or water
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • ½ tsp sesame oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Spring onions (for garnish)
  • A few bok choy or cabbage leaves (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare filling:
    • Mix minced meat, spring onions, soy sauce, ginger, garlic, salt, and pepper.

  2. Make wontons:
    • Place a small spoon of filling in the center of a wrapper.
    • Fold and seal edges with a little water.

  3. Cook wontons:
    • Boil water and cook wontons for 3–4 minutes until they float. Remove and set aside.

  4. Prepare soup:
    • Heat chicken stock, add soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and vegetables if using.

  5. Combine:
    • Add cooked wontons to the soup and simmer for 2 minutes.

  6. Serve hot:
    • Garnish with spring onions.

You can add noodles or shrimp for extra flavor.
