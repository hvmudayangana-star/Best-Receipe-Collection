Kiribath

Kiribath is a traditional Sri Lankan dish made by cooking rice with coconut milk until it becomes soft, creamy, and slightly sticky.

Once cooked, it is usually spread on a tray, shaped, and cut into diamond or square pieces.

Kiribath is an important ceremonial food in Sri Lanka and is commonly served during:

  • Sinhala & Tamil New Year
  • Birthdays
  • Weddings
  • Religious or auspicious events

It is typically enjoyed with lunu miris (spicy onion sambol) or sometimes with jaggery for a sweet version.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups white raw rice (short grain preferred)
  • 1½ cups thick coconut milk
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Wash the rice well and place it in a pot with 4 cups of water.
  2. Boil and cook the rice on medium heat until soft and water is mostly absorbed.
  3. Add salt and coconut milk, stir gently, and cook on low heat for another 5–10 minutes until thick and creamy.
  4. Remove from heat and flatten the kiribath on a tray or banana leaf.
  5. Let it cool slightly, then cut into diamond or square shapes.
