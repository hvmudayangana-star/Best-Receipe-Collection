Coconut Sambol

Coconut sambol (Pol Sambol) is a popular Sri Lankan side dish made from freshly grated coconut, mixed with chili, onion, lime juice, salt, and sometimes a bit of Maldive fish for extra flavor.

It has a spicy, tangy taste and is commonly eaten with:

  • Rice and curry
  • Kiribath
  • String hoppersv
  • Hoppers
  • Roti

Coconut sambol is simple, flavorful, and one of the most iconic accompaniments in Sri Lankan cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup freshly grated coconut (or desiccated, soaked)
  • 1 small red onion (finely chopped)
  • 1–2 green or red chilies (sliced)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp salt (or to taste)
  • Juice of ½ a lime or small lemon
  • Optional: ½ tsp Maldive fish flakes (for authentic flavor)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix grated coconut, onion, chilies, chili powder, and salt.
  2. Add lime juice and mix everything well using your fingers or a spoon.
  3. (Optional) Add Maldive fish flakes for extra flavor.
  4. Taste and adjust salt or lime as needed.
