Kottu Roti

Kottu roti (also called Koththu Roti) is a popular Sri Lankan street food made by chopping flatbread (godamba roti) and mixing it with other ingredients on a hot griddle.

Key points about Kottu Roti:

  • Made using godamba roti, vegetables, eggs, and spices.
  • Can include chicken, beef, seafood, or cheese.
  • Known for the loud chopping sound made by metal blades while cooking.
  • Spicy, flavorful, and very filling.
  • Commonly eaten for dinner or supper.

Kottu roti is one of the most famous and loved foods in Sri Lanka.

Ingredients

  • 4–5 Godamba rotis
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, beef, egg, or tofu (optional)
  • 1 cup cabbage (shredded)
  • 1 carrot (grated or julienned)
  • 1 small onion (sliced)
  • 2 garlic cloves (chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (sliced)
  • 2 eggs (optional)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil

Instructions

  1. Chop cooked godamba roti into thin strips.
  2. Heat oil in a large pan. Add onions, garlic, and green chilies. Sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add cabbage, carrot, and any meat or egg. Stir-fry for 2–3 mins.
  4. If using eggs, scramble them in the pan now.
  5. Add the chopped roti and mix everything well. Stir-fry for 3–5 minutes until fully combined and heated through.

Serve hot with chili paste or curry sauce. It’s spicy, flavorful, and a true Sri Lankan favorite!
