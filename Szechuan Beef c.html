Szechuan Beef

Szechuan Beef (also spelled Sichuan Beef) is a spicy Chinese dish from Sichuan cuisine.

Key points about Szechuan Beef:

  • Made with thinly sliced beef stir-fried with vegetables.
  • Uses Sichuan peppercorns and chili peppers, giving a spicy and numbing (málà) taste.
  • Cooked with garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and chili paste.
  • Bold, fiery flavor with a slightly smoky aroma.

Szechuan Beef is commonly served hot with steamed rice and is popular for its strong, spicy taste.

Ingredients

    For the beef:

  • 300g boneless chicken (cut into small cubes)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp Shaoxing wine or dry sherry (optional)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

    For the sauce:

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce (for color, optional)
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp chili paste or Szechuan chili sauce
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 3 tbsp water

    Other ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp Szechuan peppercorns (lightly crushed, optional for numbing spice)
  • 4–6 dried red chilies (cut in half)
  • 1 red or green bell pepper (sliced)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 tsp ginger (minced)
  • 2–3 tbsp oil for stir-frying

Instructions

  1. Marinate the beef:
    • Mix sliced beef with soy sauce, cornstarch, sesame oil, and vinegar. Let sit 15 minutes.

  2. Mix the sauce:
    • In a bowl, combine all sauce ingredients and set aside.

  3. Stir-fry the beef:
    • Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok. Stir-fry beef until browned. Remove and set aside.

  4. Cook aromatics:
    • Add more oil. Stir-fry garlic, ginger, dried chilies, and peppercorns briefly.
    • Add onions and bell peppers. Cook until slightly tender.

  5. Combine all:
    • Return beef to the pan. Add sauce and stir until thickened and glossy.

Serve hot with steamed rice.
