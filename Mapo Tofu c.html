Mapo Tofu

Mapo Tofu is a famous Chinese dish from Sichuan cuisine.

Key points about Mapo Tofu:

  • Made with soft tofu cooked in a spicy, savory sauce.
  • The sauce includes chili bean paste (doubanjiang), garlic, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns.
  • Often contains minced meat (usually pork or beef).
  • Known for its spicy, numbing (málà) flavor.

Mapo tofu is commonly eaten with steamed rice and is loved for its bold taste and soft texture.

Ingredients

  • 300g soft or medium-firm tofu (cubed)
  • 150g ground pork or beef
  • 2 tbsp Sichuan doubanjiang (chili bean paste)
  • 1 tbsp fermented black beans (optional)
  • 2–3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 tsp ginger (minced)
  • 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns (crushed, optional for numbing spice)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp cornstarch + 2 tbsp water (for thickening)
  • 2 spring onions (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Cooked rice (to serve)

Instructions

  1. Prepare tofu:
    • Gently boil tofu cubes in salted water for 2–3 minutes. Drain and set aside.

  2. Cook aromatics:
    • Heat oil in a pan. Add garlic, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns. Stir-fry until fragrant.

  3. Add meat & sauce:
    • Add ground meat and cook until browned.
    • Stir in doubanjiang, black beans (if using), soy sauce, and sugar. Cook 1–2 mins.

  4. Add tofu & simmer:
    • Gently add tofu and broth. Simmer for 5 minutes.

  5. Thicken sauce:
    • Mix cornstarch with water. Add to the pan and stir gently until the sauce thickens.

  6. Serve
    • Garnish with spring onions. Serve hot with rice.

Serve hot.
