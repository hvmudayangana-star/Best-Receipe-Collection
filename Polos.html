JackFruit

JackFruit (Polos) is a traditional Sri Lankan dish made from young (unripe) jackfruit. At this stage, the jackfruit is firm and not sweet, making it perfect for savory curries.

Key features of polos:

  • It is usually cooked as a thick, spicy curry with roasted spices, coconut milk, and seasoning.
  • The texture becomes soft and tender when cooked, similar to meat, which makes it a popular vegetarian/vegan option.
  • Polos curry is commonly eaten with rice and is a staple in Sri Lankan home cooking.

In short, polos refers to unripe jackfruit and the delightful curry made from it.

Ingredients

  • 500g young jackfruit (polos), peeled and cut into cubes
  • 1 medium onion (sliced)
  • 2–3 garlic cloves (sliced)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1–2 pandan leaves (rampa)
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 1 tbsp roasted curry powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp black pepper (optional)
  • ½ tsp salt (or to taste)
  • 1–2 pieces goraka (or 1 tbsp tamarind paste)
  • 1½ cups thick coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil or vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds and let them pop.
  2. Add onion, garlic, curry leaves, and pandan leaf. Sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the jackfruit pieces and all the spices:
    • curry powder
    • turmeric
    • chili
    • pepper
    • Salt
  4. Add goraka or tamarind, and pour in enough water to cover the jackfruit. Simmer for 20–30 mins until tender.
  5. Add coconut milk and simmer on low heat for another 10–15 minutes until curry thickens.
